Chris Christophis

Film and Opera Director - Playwright - Lyricist


Born in Alexandria, he was raised in Athens where he lives and works, dividing his professional time between Greece, Italy and France. After finishing the lyceum, he studied film, theater and the sociology of the ancient Greek world at Sorbonne (Ecole Pratique des Hautes Etudes, France) and served as an assistant to Gian Carlo Menoti and Lila de Nobili at Spoletto (Italy). He directed «Wandering» his first feature-length film at the age of 23.


Chris Christophis by Lila De Nobili – Portraits in Oil - Paris

Introduction

Chris Christophis-Pittas was born in Alexandria, grew up in Athens, studied in Paris, where he lived for several years, as well as in Italy and New York. In the cinema he is the author of the well known movies "Wandering", "Roza", "Monkey Queen".

With his two first films he had international recognition in Cannes, Rotterdam, Mannheim and New York Festivals. Received warm welcoming from the foreign press. Awarded twice at the Thessaloniki Festival.

For the theater he presented his work "The Comedy of Mythomania", "The Orators" and "Mayfly", a project that also aired in English at «Old Vic» London 2000.

For television he has directed the series "Lost Spring" from the novel by Strati Tsirka and "Vizyinos-Silence of Angels", which won the big prizes of television in 2000. In the field of opera and musical theater he directed performances for the Opera of Tours in France, and also in Ascoli Italy with works such as "The Temptations of St. Anthony", with music by M. Kuzan and "Me, Pinocchio" by A. Tserikola, both projects world premiere. In Greece he has directed the musical works of G. Drositi (The Conductor Konmpravoura), N. Xanthouli (The Tears of the Moon) and the stage cantata by P. Koukou (Diary of Passerby) at his own libretto. He continues to work in Italy and recently in Lisbon.
